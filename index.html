LeBron James, the Greatest of All Time

Born and raised in Akron, Ohio, LeBron James is regarded as one of the best basketball players to bless the planet. With:

  • 4 NBA championships
  • 4 MVPs
  • 4 Final MVPs
  • 6 All Defensive Teams
  • 21 All NBA
  • 3 Olympic Gold Medals
Calling LeBron James the Greatest of All Time (G.O.A.T) would be an understatement. From The Decision, to winning in Miami and coming back to Clevaland for another, to the 2016 3-1 comeback, Lakers Bubble Ring, and countless Olympic Medals, only time can slow him down. Despite this, at the age of 40, he stands alone as the single most accomplished and longest tenured basketball player.

Over his decorated career, LeBron has faced every team countless times, with most being victorious. He seems to have a certain aura to him, with some even giving him nicknames due to his consistent dominance over certain teams: LeBronto is one to signal his triumphs over the Toronto Raptors

Being the oldest current player in the NBA has not stopped LeBron, with consistency being a large component of his legacy. He has not dropped in any major statistical category over his 22 years of playing in the league, and becoming the All-Time Points Leader in February 7th, 2023, overtaking Kareem Abdul-Jabbar with 38387 points

While some compare him to other players to diminish his reputation as the Greatest of All Time, certain things are just not comparable. LeBron, while easily being the oldest in the league, still manages to fit himself into the top 15 points scorer in the previous season. Furthermore, unlike greats of the past, his decline, or lack there of, is completely unheard of compared to other Hall of Famers like Michael Jorden, Larry Bird, and Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
